Hi I'm Alex!

I am a final year mechanical engineering student currently studying at Imperial College London. This is a page that is dedicated to the projects that I have worked on as a mechanical engineer and a developer over my career. My ethos as an engineer is to develop systems that changes lives in a positive way. As a result, I became most passionate about Medical Technologies, Robotics and Automation, as well as XR. Below are a selection of projects that I have worked on, and I am excited to share them with you!

Monte Carlo Simulations for predicting ablation path for LiTT

LiTT stands for laser interstitial thermal therapy, and it is a procedure used to treat epilepsy. Partnering with the EDEN 2020 project, a Monte Carlo based laser model is being developed to find the optimal path for a soft robot needle to minimize thermal damage.

Open Source Medical Robot

OSMR stands for open source medical robot, and is an initiative that aims to create a collaborative and low cost tendon driven continuum robot for endoscopic surgery.

Augmented reality for malleable robot

A robot with rigid links cannot possible reach everywhere in a workspace due to singularities and other constraints. But what about a malleable robot?

Signature Robot

Performing accurate knee replacement (Arthoplasty) is a dangerous operation in orthopaedic surgeries. This can be mitigated via a cleverly designed robot that utilizes active constraints, and small wrist motions, just like signing with a pen.

Autonomous vehicle for bomb disposal

Final project for my course on Embedded C for Microcontrollers course, used to difuse a hypothetical bomb at Imperial.

Mechatronics Labs via Simulink

As a final year student, I supported the lecturers of the second year mechatronics course to rewrite their curriculum and making it suitable for remote learning.
