Open Source Medical Robot

Minimally invasive surgery is a field with constant interest due to its notable benefits for the patient, such as faster recovery times, less pain and less collateral damage. With the implementation of continuum and soft robots into the workflow, surgeons are no longer restricted to conduct delicate movements within the patient's body. However, the development within soft robotics and continuum robots specifically for the medical field has been stagnant, where the high cost of such devices halting many researchers from entering the field.

With this motivation in mind, the REDS robotics lab at Imperial has developed a low cost 3D printed plastic continuum robot that can be assembled in only a few hours. Despite many imperfections in the design such as slack in the actuation tendons and the poor elastic nature of 3D printing polymers, this robot acts as a stepping stone towards more refined designs and has to potential to enable indeviduals to contribute to such a system.

Having said that, the original project did not consider in detail the forward and inverse kinematics of the robot manipulator, and thus control was not possible. Since I have joined the project, I have been working to devise a "simple" and reliable algorithm which enables kinematic control on this system. This will then be implemented via an Arduino and numerous servo motors. More details are coming in the near future!
